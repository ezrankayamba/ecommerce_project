Masai people

The Maasai are a Nilotic largest group inhabiting northern, central and southern Kenya and northern Tanzania. They are among the best known local populations internationally due to their residence in towns and villages near the many game parks of the African Great Lakes, and their distinctive customs and dress.[4] The Maasai speak the Maa language (É”l Maa),[4] a member of the Nilo-Saharan family that is related to the Dinka, Kalenjin and Nuer languages. Many have become educated in the official languages of Kenya and Tanzania, Swahili and English. The Maasai population has been reported as numbering 2,841,622 in Kenya in the 2009 census,[1] compared to 1,377,089 in the 1989 census.
